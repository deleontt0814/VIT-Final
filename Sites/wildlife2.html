Sea otters are members of the weasel or mustelid family. Like other members of this family,
they have very thick fur. In fact, at 850,000 to one million hairs per square inch, they have the
thickest fur of any mammal. Their fur actually consists of two layers, an undercoat and longer guard hairs.
This system traps a layer of air next to their skin so their skin does not get wet. Sea otters are usually dark brown,
often with lighter guard hairs. Alaskan sea otters tend to have lighter fur on their heads.
